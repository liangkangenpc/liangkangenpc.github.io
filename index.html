About

I am a transportation and urban systems researcher specializing in home-to-work commuting, spatial interaction modeling, transport econometrics, and transport–environment coupling. My doctoral research develops an integrated analytical framework linking commuting rhythms, urban spatial structure, and environmental impacts in France.

My work bridges gravity models, discrete choice models, and entropy maximization, with applications in policy simulation, job accessibility, and carbon emissions.
